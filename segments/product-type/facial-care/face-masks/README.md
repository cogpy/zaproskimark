# Face Masks

Treatment products applied to the face for specific skincare benefits, delivering concentrated ingredients for intensive care and visible results.

## Product Categories

### Sheet Masks
Single-use fabric masks soaked in serum or essence, popular in K-beauty and Asian skincare.

#### Key Features
- Pre-cut fabric fitted to face
- Saturated with concentrated serum
- Convenient, mess-free application
- Various fabric materials (cotton, bio-cellulose, hydrogel)
- Single-use, hygienic

#### Variations
- **Cotton Sheet Masks**: Most common, affordable
- **Bio-Cellulose Masks**: Superior adherence, cooling
- **Hydrogel Masks**: Gel-based, hydrating, cooling
- **Foil Masks**: Heat-trapping for better penetration

### Clay Masks
Mineral-rich formulations that absorb excess oil and impurities.

#### Key Features
- Deep pore cleansing
- Oil absorption
- Detoxifying properties
- Typically rinse-off
- Best for oily/combination skin

#### Types of Clay
- **Kaolin Clay**: Gentle, suitable for sensitive skin
- **Bentonite Clay**: Strong absorption, oily skin
- **French Green Clay**: Purifying, detoxifying
- **Pink Clay**: Gentle, combination skin
- **Dead Sea Mud**: Mineral-rich, therapeutic

### Cream Masks
Rich, emollient formulations providing intensive hydration and nourishment.

#### Key Features
- Deep moisturization
- Barrier repair
- Suitable for dry, mature skin
- Leave-on or rinse-off
- Luxurious, spa-like experience

### Gel Masks
Water-based, cooling formulations for hydration and soothing.

#### Key Features
- Lightweight, refreshing
- Intense hydration
- Cooling, calming effect
- Suitable for all skin types
- Often transparent or blue-tinted

### Peel-Off Masks
Formulations that dry and are removed by peeling.

#### Key Features
- Physical removal of dead skin
- Pore-cleansing benefits
- Satisfying removal process
- Can contain charcoal or other actives
- Not suitable for sensitive skin

### Sleeping Masks
Overnight treatments left on while sleeping.

#### Key Features
- No rinsing required
- Work while you sleep
- Intensive hydration or treatment
- Time-release technology
- Wake up with refreshed skin

### Exfoliating Masks
Formulations containing chemical or physical exfoliants.

#### Key Features
- Remove dead skin cells
- Brighten and smooth
- Improve texture
- Use 1-2 times per week
- May contain AHA, BHA, or enzymes

### Bubble/Foam Masks
Innovative formulations that create foam or bubbles on the skin.

#### Key Features
- Oxygenating properties
- Deep cleansing action
- Fun, interactive experience
- Brightening effects
- Instagram-worthy

## Usage Guidelines

### Application Frequency

#### Sheet Masks
- 1-3 times per week
- Can be used daily for intensive treatment
- Before special events

#### Clay Masks
- 1-2 times per week for oily skin
- Once per week for combination skin
- Every 2 weeks for dry/sensitive skin

#### Sleeping Masks
- 2-3 times per week
- Can replace night moisturizer
- Use after full evening routine

#### Exfoliating Masks
- 1-2 times per week
- Not on consecutive days
- Avoid if using other exfoliants

### Application Technique

#### Sheet Masks
1. Apply to clean, toned skin
2. Adjust to fit facial contours
3. Leave on 15-20 minutes
4. Remove and pat in remaining serum
5. No need to rinse

#### Rinse-Off Masks (Clay, Cream)
1. Apply to clean skin
2. Avoid eye and lip areas
3. Leave on recommended time (10-20 min)
4. Rinse with lukewarm water
5. Follow with toner and moisturizer

#### Sleeping Masks
1. Apply as last step of night routine
2. Thin, even layer
3. Allow to absorb before bed
4. Rinse off in morning

### Timing Recommendations

#### Best Time to Use
- **Evening**: Most masks (skin repairs at night)
- **Morning**: Quick hydrating sheet masks
- **Before Events**: 1-2 days prior for best results
- **Weekly Ritual**: Sunday self-care

#### Duration
- **Sheet Masks**: 15-20 minutes (don't leave until dry)
- **Clay Masks**: 10-15 minutes (don't let completely dry)
- **Cream Masks**: 15-20 minutes
- **Sleeping Masks**: Overnight (6-8 hours)

## Multi-Masking

### Concept
Using different masks on different facial zones to address varied skin concerns simultaneously.

### Typical Combinations
- **T-Zone**: Clay mask (oily areas)
- **Cheeks**: Hydrating mask (dry areas)
- **Under Eyes**: Brightening or anti-aging
- **Forehead**: Purifying or exfoliating

### Benefits
- Targeted treatment
- Addresses combination skin
- Maximizes results
- Customized approach

## Storage and Shelf Life

### Best Practices
- Store sheet masks in cool, dry place
- Refrigerate for extra cooling effect
- Keep jar masks sealed tightly
- Use within 6-12 months of opening
- Check expiration dates

### Travel Tips
- Sheet masks: TSA-friendly, convenient
- Transfer jar masks to travel containers
- Individual packets ideal for travel
- Store in sealed bags to prevent leaks
